Bardolino

Bardolino is an Italian red wine produced along the chain of morainic hills in the province of Verona to the east of Lake Garda. It takes its name from the town Bardolino on the shores of Lake Garda and was awarded Denominazione di origine controllata status in 1968.

Trentino Wines

Pinot Grigio Trentino

Pinot Grigio has been grown in Trentino for decades. In the pedecollinari and valley vineyards - now converted to organic viticulture - of Lavis and Gardolo, on the Franco-sandy lands originating from the deposits of the Avisio torrent and from the alluvial sediments of the Adige river, Pinot Grigio finds the ideal substratum. At the organoleptic analysis, the wine has an intense golden yellow color, with a warm and inviting aroma, rich in ripe fruit and honey. The rich minerality stands out in the mouth, with a long and fruity aftertaste.
